Why Sellers Don't Respond

  • The SSP asks sellers to provide a UCC-3 termination statement OR a signed letter from the secured party confirming good standing and authorization for additional debt
  • This requires sellers to contact their existing lender and request documentation — a process that can take weeks
  • The 14-day SSP response window may be insufficient for this type of documentation
  • Many sellers may not understand what a UCC filing is or how to obtain the required documents

Proposal

Proposal

Stop sending Additional Debt Allowance SSPs for loans under $250K. SFS does not file UCC liens against businesses for loans below this threshold, so requiring sellers to obtain permission from existing secured parties is unnecessary for these loan sizes.

Of the 53 canceled applications, the majority had offer amounts well below $250K. Removing this SSP requirement for sub-$250K loans would:

  • Recover a significant portion of the $6.9M lost pipeline
  • Reduce reviewer workload and SSP round-trips
  • Improve seller experience for our highest-quality borrowers
  • Reserve the Additional Debt Allowance SSP for loans ≥$250K where SFS has a direct lien interest
For Loans ≥$250K

Continue sending the Additional Debt Allowance SSP, but improve the SSP language to better explain what documentation is needed and where to obtain it.
